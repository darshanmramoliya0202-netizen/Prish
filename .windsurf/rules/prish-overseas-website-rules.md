# Prish Overseas Website Rules

## Objective
Build a premium, emotionally resonant, highly interactive export website for Prish Overseas that feels rooted in India and ready for global B2B buyers.

## Non-Negotiable Creative Rules
- The website must not look like a brochure-to-web conversion.
- The brochure may guide content, proof points, and product information only.
- The visual language must feel original, premium, and digital-first.
- Avoid generic corporate import-export layouts.
- Avoid brochure color blocking, brochure page compositions, and brochure section mimicry.
- Represent the spirit of India through atmosphere, motion, craft, and storytelling rather than clichés.
- Every major section must combine emotion with commercial trust.

## Brand Direction
- Rooted in Indian agricultural richness
- Global in execution quality
- Premium in typography and spacing
- Interactive in motion and narrative flow
- Trust-led for international B2B decision makers

## Content Priorities
- Lead with origin, traceability, and formulation relevance
- Highlight export capability, quality systems, and scalable supply
- Present products as a curated ingredient portfolio, not as a crowded catalog
- Keep claims aligned with brochure facts unless explicitly approved by the user
- Polish brochure language before using it on the website

## UX Rules
- Make the homepage feel cinematic and memorable within the first screen
- Keep navigation simple and conversion-oriented
- Maintain clear inquiry paths for importers, distributors, and brand owners
- Use strong trust markers early in the journey
- Ensure the site is responsive, performant, and accessible

## Build Rules
- Prefer reusable sections and clean data-driven structures
- Keep content, layout, and styling modular where possible
- Use premium motion sparingly and intentionally
- Preserve readability while creating a distinctive aesthetic
- Do not add filler pages or placeholder sections without purpose

## Change Tracking Rules
- Every code change must be recorded in `CHANGE_LOG.md`
- Each log entry must include date and timestamp
- Each entry must include affected file paths, summary, reason, and impact
- Large edits must be logged as separate entries if they affect distinct areas
- If a change revises content strategy or design direction, log that explicitly

## Execution Phases
1. Establish rules, skills, and logging
2. Scaffold the application foundation
3. Build a unique homepage direction
4. Expand reusable sections and supporting content
5. Refine responsiveness, performance, and trust content
6. Prepare for testing and deployment
